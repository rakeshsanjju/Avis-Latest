What are Varicose Veins?

Varicose veins are swollen, twisted blood vessels that appear just beneath the skin, usually on the legs, feet, or ankles. They often look blue or purple and may feel painful or itchy. Smaller veins, called spider veins, can also develop nearby and appear as fine red or purple lines on the skin.

While varicose veins are usually not dangerous, they can be uncomfortable or bothersome in appearance. In severe cases, they may cause complications such as blood clots. Many symptoms can be managed at home, but more serious cases may require medical treatments like injections, laser therapy, or surgery.

Common Symptoms

Swollen, twisted veins (legs)
Pain, heaviness, itching
Night cramps, skin discoloration

Why Treatment is Needed

Ignoring varicose veins can lead to serious health problems that worsen over time. Early intervention helps prevent complications and improves overall quality of life.

Risk 1

Worsening pain and swelling

Risk 2

Skin ulcers and bleeding

Risk 3

Risk of blood clots (DVT)
